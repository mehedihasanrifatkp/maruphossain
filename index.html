I am an applied economist working on contemporary agricultural and development issues. I am currently working as an economist in the research and impact assessment division at the International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD). In addition to my primary job at IFAD, I work as an impact assessment and analytics specialist at the UN Capital Development Fund (UNCDF). I have a Ph.D. in applied economics from the University of Florida, USA.

My research areas are access to finance, entrepreneurship, and resilience. I conduct randomized control trials and ex-post impact assessment studies to evaluate different development interventions. I also work on measurement and targeting effectiveness issues using different econometric and supervised machine learning tools. My research works have been published in the American Journal of Agricultural Economics, Development Policy Review, Food Policy, Food Security, and World Development.
